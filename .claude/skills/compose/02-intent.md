# Step 2: Develop Intent

**Goal:** Build the argument or brief BEFORE any copy exists. This is the checkpoint that prevents bad structure from embedding. The depth of intent development scales with the format.

---

## Rules

- Never skip intent development, even for "quick" emails. The light version takes 30 seconds and catches bad framing before it embeds.
- Every element grounded in loaded source docs. Ungrounded claims get flagged.
- The operator approves the intent before any composition happens. This is the highest-leverage feedback moment.

---

## Instructions

### For Email Format (Light Intent)

Produce a **Brief** — enough to align on purpose, not a full persuasive argument:

```
TO: [Recipients + roles]
FROM: [Send account]
PURPOSE: [One sentence — what this email does. "Recap the weekly sync + request document review."]
KEY INFO: [2-4 bullets — the substance. What the reader needs to know or do.]
TONE: [One line — register calibration. "Peer recap, not formal report." "Direct ask, short."]
THREAD: [New / Reply to [subject] / Forward of [subject]]
```

For post-debrief emails, pre-populate from the debrief output — recipients, key points, action items are already in the conversation.

Present the brief. Operator confirms or adjusts.

### For Data Writeup Format (Medium Intent)

Produce a **Writeup Brief:**

```
READER: [Who reads this. What they need to decide or understand.]
HEADLINE: [The single most important finding or result. This becomes the opening.]
KEY DATA: [3-5 data points that carry the story. These are the proof.]
ASSESSMENT: [Your honest read — what worked, what didn't, what's uncertain.]
DECISIONS: [What the reader needs to decide, if anything. Frame as THEIR decisions.]
LENGTH: [Target — "1.5 pages" / "500 words" / "enough for a 10-minute read"]
```

Present. Operator confirms or adjusts.

### For Persuasive Format (Full Copy Intent)

Produce a **Copy Intent** — tuned for persuasion:

```
READER: Who reads this. What they currently believe. Their emotional
state on arrival (frustrated, curious, skeptical, hopeful). What's
competing for their attention.

TRANSFORMATION: What should they believe AFTER? The specific belief
shift. Not "they should want our product" — "they should realize their
current approach is costing them 80% of their potential output."

EMOTIONAL ARC: The reader's journey from entry to action. Map it:
recognition → frustration → possibility → clarity → urgency. Each stage
is a waypoint the copy must move them through.

KEY MOVES: The 3-5 persuasive moves that carry the argument. Each move
is a belief shift. Order matters — what must they believe first before
the next move lands? Moves are not sections. Moves are what the copy
DOES at each stage.

PROOF: For each key move, what makes it credible? Pull from loaded
context — real results, specific examples, operator's experience,
social proof, third-party validation. If proof doesn't exist in the
docs, flag: "We need proof for [claim] — what do you have?"

VOICE: Tonal register drawn from source material and voice stack.
What does this sound like? What does it NOT sound like?
```

**Rules for the Copy Intent:**
- This is NOT an outline. No section headers. No paragraph plan.
- This is NOT a draft. No pre-written sentences.
- Keep it to 15-25 lines. Longer means it's doing too much.
- Every element grounded in loaded source docs. Ungrounded claims get flagged.

Present the Copy Intent. Operator confirms or adjusts.

### For All Formats: The Gate

The operator can:
- **Approve** — "go write it"
- **Adjust** — "the angle is wrong" / "you're missing [X]" / "tone should be more [Y]"
- **Redirect** — "wrong approach entirely"

Adjusting a 10-line intent takes 30 seconds. Rewriting 8 sections of copy takes 45 minutes. This gate is where the leverage is.

**Do not proceed to Step 3 until the operator approves the intent.**

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Intent produced at the appropriate depth for the format
- [ ] Every claim/element grounded in loaded source material
- [ ] Operator reviewed and approved the intent
