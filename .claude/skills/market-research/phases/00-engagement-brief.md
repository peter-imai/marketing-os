# Phase 0: Engagement Brief — Interview Framework

> **When to load:** At the start of market research. This guides the operator interview in main context.
> **Purpose:** Capture what the operator and client team already know BEFORE any model research begins. Seeds all subsequent phases and creates a baseline for "what we assumed vs. what we found."

---

## How to Use This

Conduct a structured interview with the operator. This is a conversation, not a form. Use the questions below as a framework — adapt the flow based on what the operator says. Follow threads when they're interesting. Skip questions the operator clearly can't answer yet.

If the operator has intake from the client team (e.g., a partner filled out a brief), read that first, then use this interview to fill gaps and add the operator's own read.

---

## Interview Framework

### The Business

- What does [client] sell? What problem does it solve? In plain language, not marketing language.
- Who buys it? What's their title, their company size, their situation?
- What's the pricing model? What does a typical deal look like?
- How mature is the product? What's strong, what's weak, what's missing?

### The Landscape

- Who do they compete with? Name companies if you can.
- What do prospects usually do instead of buying this? *(Probe for the do-nothing competitor — spreadsheets, manual workarounds, ignoring the problem.)*
- What's the market like right now? Growing, consolidating, commoditizing?
- Are there any big trends or events shaping the market right now?

### The State of Play

- What's working in their current GTM? What's not?
- What have they tried before? What failed and why?
- Where do their leads come from today?
- What's their sales process like?

### The Relationship

- Who are the key people we're working with? What are they like?
- What are they optimizing for? (Growth? Efficiency? Survival?)
- What are they worried about that they might not say directly?
- What does success look like for this engagement — from their perspective AND ours?

### The Operator's Read

- What's your gut feeling about this market/opportunity?
- What's the biggest opportunity you see?
- What could go wrong?
- What do you wish you knew but don't yet?

---

## Output Format

After the interview, synthesize responses into a structured brief:

```markdown
# Engagement Brief: [Client Name]

## Business Overview
[What they sell, who buys, pricing, product maturity — in the operator's words]

## Competitive Landscape (Operator's View)
[Named competitors, do-nothing alternatives, market dynamics — as the operator understands them today]

## Current GTM State
[What's working, what's not, lead sources, sales process]

## Engagement Context
[Key stakeholders, what they're optimizing for, concerns, success criteria]

## Operator's Hypotheses
[Gut read on the market, biggest opportunity, risks, knowledge gaps]

## Key Questions to Investigate
[Specific questions that emerged from the interview — these seed the research phases]

## Baseline Assumptions
[Explicit list of assumptions to test against research findings]
```

Save to: `_inputs/research/00-engagement-brief.md` in the workspace context directory.
