# Phase 4.5: Research Synthesis — Output Template

> **When to load:** After all core research phases (0-4) are complete. Guides the synthesis in main context.
> **Purpose:** This is the template for `market-research.md` — the primary deliverable that gets loaded in every future session.

---

## What This Document Is

`market-research.md` is not a stapling of prior phases. It's a synthesis — the strategic layer that sits on top of the detailed research. Someone should be able to read this document in 5 minutes and understand the market, the buyers, the competition, and what it means for approach.

The detailed phase deliverables (`icp.md`, `competitive-landscape.md`, `buyer-personas.md`) still exist for deep reference. This document is the navigational summary.

## Template

```markdown
# Market Research: [Client Name]

*Synthesized from [N] research phases. Last updated [date].*
*Source confidence key: [H] = high confidence, [M] = needs qualification, [L] = do not claim*

## Executive Summary

[3-5 paragraphs. Written for someone who needs to understand this market in 2 minutes. Cover: what the company does, who buys it, what the market looks like right now, who the competition is, and the 2-3 things that matter most for go-to-market. This should read like a strategic briefing, not a research report.]

## ICP Summary

**Target Profile:** [One-paragraph description of the ideal customer]

**Key Signals:**
- Firmographic: [Revenue range, employee count, industry, geography]
- Technographic: [Technology stack indicators, integration requirements]
- Behavioral: [Buying signals, trigger events, timing indicators]

**Disqualifying Signals:** [Who to NOT target — and why]

**Confidence:** [What's solid vs. inferred in the ICP]

## Market Dynamics

[2-3 paragraphs. Where the market sits in its adoption lifecycle. What's happening NOW that creates urgency or opportunity. How the market stage affects buying behavior. Any time-sensitive signals.]

**Key Takeaway:** [One sentence — the single most important market dynamic for GTM decisions]

## Competitive Reality

**Named Competitors:**
[For each significant competitor (3-7): one paragraph covering who they are, where they're strong, where they're vulnerable, and the displacement angle. Include competitive matrix.]

| Competitor | Primary Strength | Key Weakness | Switching Friction | Best Displacement Angle |
|------------|-----------------|-------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| ... | ... | ... | H/M/L | ... |

**The Do-Nothing Segment:**
[1-2 paragraphs. What workarounds prospects use today, why inertia persists, true cost of doing nothing. This is often 50-70% of the addressable market.]

**Trigger Events:**
[Top 3-5 trigger events that break inertia, organized by tier. For each: what it looks like and how to detect it.]

## Buyer Profiles

[For each persona (2-4): one paragraph covering who they are, their current situation, what keeps them up at night, and the best approach to reach them.]

**Priority:** [Which persona is the primary buyer and why]
**Role Map:** [Decision-maker / Influencer / Evaluator / Blocker for each]

## Strategic Implications

[5-8 numbered items. "Given what we know, here's what this means for how we approach this engagement..." These should be specific, actionable, and connect research to decisions. Examples:]

[1. "The do-nothing segment is our biggest opportunity because..."]
[2. "Messaging should lead with [specific pain] because..."]
[3. "The [trigger event] creates the best outbound timing because..."]
[4. "We should NOT compete on [dimension] because..."]
[5. "Phase 0 assumption about [X] was wrong — the research shows..."]

## Source Confidence Summary

**Claim with confidence:**
- [Claims backed by multiple credible, recent sources]

**Claim with qualification:**
- [Claims from single sources or older data — state as "evidence suggests" or "our research indicates"]

**Do not claim:**
- [Areas where evidence is weak, contradictory, or missing — flag what would resolve these]

## Open Questions

[What we still don't know. What needs validation from the client. What would change the strategy if we learned it. Each question should explain WHY it matters — not just what's unknown.]
```

## Writing Principles

- **Narrative over bullets.** Paragraphs for analysis. Tables for quick reference. Bullets only for lists of discrete items.
- **Conviction over hedging.** "The primary opportunity is..." not "One potential opportunity could be..."
- **Specifics over generalities.** Name companies, name titles, name dollar amounts, name timelines.
- **Connection over listing.** Every section should connect to the others. ICP connects to competitive reality. Competitive reality connects to trigger events. Trigger events connect to personas. Personas connect to strategic implications.
- **Phase 0 callback.** In Strategic Implications, explicitly compare findings against the baseline assumptions from the engagement brief. What was right? What was wrong? What was missing?
